### 1. The Big Picture: Where Are We?

The core objective of this project is to **utilize an LLM to read 28 scientific papers and automatically extract CMOCs (Context - Mechanism - Outcome Configurations) matching the human-derived results of the Richmond (2020) review.**

To achieve this, the project is structured into three distinct phases:
- **Phase 1: Zero-shot Extraction:** Feeding 28 papers to the AI for initial, unguided data extraction. *(Status: Complete. Output stored in `evidence_table.json`).*
- **Phase 2: Evaluation Framework & HITL (Human-in-the-loop):** Building a measurement system to benchmark the AI against the human standard (Richmond), and establishing a mechanism for human oversight. *(Status: Complete. This encompasses our most recent work).*
- **Phase 3: Refinement:** Utilizing the established metrics to iteratively restructure prompts and architecture, forcing the AI to achieve higher accuracy. *(Status: Pending).*

📍 **Current Position:** We have successfully completed **Phase 2**.

### 2. Purpose of Recent Deliverables

The scripts and output files generated recently serve three critical functions for your PhD dissertation:

1. **The Evaluator (`Phase1_Relationship_Evaluation.md`)**
   - *Purpose:* To replace vague claims of "good AI performance" with rigorous scientific metrics. This script evaluates the AI against the exact Richmond R01-R40 golden standard.
   - *Result:* The current baseline F1 score is **8%**. This low initial score is expected for a zero-shot run, and it demonstrates to your academic committee that we have a highly transparent and strict measurement tool in place.

2. **The Adjudication Template (`per_paper_adjudication_template.csv`)**
   - *Purpose:* The 8% score is a global metric. The professor requires granular knowledge of exactly *where* the AI failed in each paper. This Excel-compatible file aggregates all 57 CMOC extractions.
   - *Utility:* It pre-fills the AI's responses and leaves blank columns for **you (the researcher) to manually grade and correct** the outputs.

3. **The Human-in-the-Loop Audit Logs (`HITL_Audit_Log.md`)**
   - *Purpose:* To address the professor's concern about "blind AI execution." This log proves that the system pauses at critical milestones, awaiting human review and approval before proceeding.
