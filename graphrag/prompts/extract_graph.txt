
-Goal-
Given a text document from a medical/healthcare education study, identify all entities related to
Clinical Reasoning Education and all relationships among the identified entities.

This is for a Realist Synthesis following the CMOC framework (Context-Mechanism-Outcome Configuration)
as defined by Richmond et al. (2020): "The student is key: A realist review of educational interventions
to develop analytical and non-analytical clinical reasoning ability."

-Steps-
1. Identify all entities. For each identified entity, extract the following information:
- entity_name: Name of the entity, capitalized
- entity_type: One of the following types: [{entity_types}]
- entity_description: Comprehensive description of the entity's attributes and activities
Format each entity as ("entity"{tuple_delimiter}<entity_name>{tuple_delimiter}<entity_type>{tuple_delimiter}<entity_description>)
 
2. From the entities identified in step 1, identify all pairs of (source_entity, target_entity) that are *clearly related* to each other.
For each pair of related entities, extract the following information:
- source_entity: name of the source entity, as identified in step 1
- target_entity: name of the target entity, as identified in step 1
- relationship_description: explanation as to why you think the source entity and the target entity are related to each other
- relationship_strength: a numeric score indicating strength of the relationship between the source entity and target entity
- relationship_type: One of: PROVIDES, ENABLES, LEADS_TO, TRIGGERS, INHIBITS, MODERATES
 Format each relationship as ("relationship"{tuple_delimiter}<source_entity>{tuple_delimiter}<target_entity>{tuple_delimiter}<relationship_description>{tuple_delimiter}<relationship_strength>{tuple_delimiter}<relationship_type>)
 
3. Return output in English as a single list of all the entities and relationships identified in steps 1 and 2. Use **{record_delimiter}** as the list delimiter.
 
4. When finished, output {completion_delimiter}

-Entity Type Definitions for Medical Education Realist Synthesis-
Context: WHO is learning — the learner population, their knowledge level, coping strategies, group composition.
  Examples: "novice medical students", "Year 3 clerkship students with limited diagnostic experience", "surgical residents with existing expertise"
Intervention: WHAT the educational activity provides — the pedagogical approach or instructional design.
  Examples: "case-based self-explanation", "combined analytical and non-analytical reasoning instruction", "serious game simulation"
Mechanism_Resource: WHAT resources the intervention provides — always "multiple relevant resources" in Richmond's framework but specify what those resources are.
  Examples: "worked examples with expert commentary", "ECG practice cases with diagnostic features", "virtual patient scenarios"
Mechanism_Response: HOW the learner internally responds — the cognitive/emotional/motivational reaction.
  Examples: "students built understanding by linking biomedical and clinical knowledge", "students experienced cognitive overload", "students relied on pattern recognition"
Outcome: WHAT measurable result occurred — the learning outcome or diagnostic performance change.
  Examples: "improved diagnostic accuracy on transfer cases", "more complete illness scripts", "no reduction in diagnostic error"

Methods: Students were allocated to CFS training or control during a 4-week emergency medicine rotation. Results: There were 145 students in the intervention and 46 in the control group. For search satisficing bias cases, 52% of students with CFS training and 48% in the control group initiated a search for the second diagnosis. There were no significant differences between groups.
